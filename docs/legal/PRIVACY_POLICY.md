# Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-03-23

`Note to Self` is a local-first note-taking application published by NextEleven Studios.

## Summary

- Your notes are stored locally on your device.
- We do not run a backend service for user accounts.
- We do not sell personal data.
- We do not include third-party advertising SDKs.

## Data We Process

The app can process the following data on your device:

- Notes, tags, and categories that you create.
- Optional voice input when you choose to use speech capture.
- Optional exported files (for example, Markdown or JSON) when you choose to export.

This data is processed locally by the app and is not sent to NextEleven Studios by default.

## Permissions

Depending on your platform and features you use, the app may request:

- Microphone access (for speech-to-text capture).
- Speech recognition access (for speech transcription on supported platforms).
- File system access (for user-initiated import/export operations).

Permissions are used only for the related feature and can be revoked in device settings.

## Data Sharing

We do not share your note content with third parties.

If you export data or copy data to other apps or cloud drives, that sharing is initiated and controlled by you.

## Data Retention and Deletion

- Your data remains on your device until you delete it.
- Deleting a note in the app removes it from local storage used by the app.
- Uninstalling the app may remove local app data, subject to platform behavior and backups.

## Children's Privacy

The app is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

## Security

We rely on platform-level security controls (such as device encryption, passcodes, and OS sandboxing). You are responsible for securing your device and backups.

## International Users

Because data is stored locally by default, international transfer of your note content by us does not normally occur.

## Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material updates will be reflected by updating the effective date.
